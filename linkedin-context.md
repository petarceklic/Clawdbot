## Writing Style
- Hook-driven first line — surprising, contrarian, stops the scroll immediately
- Very short sentences. One idea per line. No fat.
- News peg → design lens → clear opinion → sharp takeaway
- Personal story → broader insight (never lecture first)
- Ends with a punchy statement, NOT a question
- Conversational, honest, slightly vulnerable when personal
- Often includes a real photo, screenshot, or video
- Hashtags: #productdesign #ux #saas #ai #freelancedesign etc.
- Never uses "I'm excited to share..." or filler phrases

## The 3 Content Pillars

### 🎯 Pillar 1 — Client Attraction
Speak to potential clients (SaaS founders, product teams, startups). Showcasing work, explaining how he works, demonstrating thought leadership, availability posts. Goal: make decision-makers think "I want this person."

### 🎨 Pillar 2 — Design-Specific
Industry tools, craft, UX philosophy, design discourse. Builds credibility with peers. Best when tied to a news peg or industry event.

### 💬 Pillar 3 — Personal Opinion / Commentary
Hot takes on tech, AI, policy, society. Doesn't need to be design-related. Widest reach. Should feel like a smart person noticing something most people are missing.

## The Viral Formula
**News peg → design/product lens → contrarian insight → sharp takeaway**

Best hooks are:
- A surprising reveal ("Except, most of the demo wasn't 'AI' at all...")
- A number that shocks ("Australia just spent $96 million on a weather website that still struggles to load")
- A provocative opener ("You do not need a university degree to become a great designer")
- A story twist ("My wife got a text on Sunday night she didn't ask for")

## What Drives Big Numbers (ranked)
1. **Tech controversy with design angle** → 40-54k (NEO, Panels, $96M weather)
2. **Honest personal contrarian take** → 8-20k (no uni, Good Design Award)
3. **Local/national policy + UX critique** → 7-12k (Transperth, weather)
4. **AI/robot future commentary** → 5-7k
5. **Industry tool hot take** → 3-5k (Framer, OpenAI Atlas, Canva)
6. **Personal AI automation story** → 2-3k
7. **Client work showcase** → 1-2k
8. **Freelance career advice** → 800-1.5k

## What to ALWAYS DO
- React to news within 24-48h while it's hot
- Take a clear stance — no "on one hand, on the other" fence-sitting
- Find the design or product angle on non-design news
- Keep posts under 250 words. Short = more readable = more reach.
- Include a visual (photo, screenshot, or video clip) when possible

## What to AVOID
- Reposts without heavy added commentary (516 impressions)
- Generic design tips lists without a news hook
- Pure promotional posts without narrative
- Posts that end with a generic question ("What do you think?")
